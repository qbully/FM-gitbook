# Reading an Alert

Every alert is built to get you from "a product dropped" to "I'm filming it" as fast as possible. Here's how to read one.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/73922-alert.png" alt="Product alert example"><figcaption>A standard product alert</figcaption></figure>

## What's In an Alert

* **Brand** — the brand behind the product
* **Product** — the full product name, including size, variant, and details
* **Commission** — the payout rate and rough dollar amount per sale, when available. Always double-check the live rate on the listing before you commit — rates can change.
* **View Product** — opens the live TikTok Shop listing so you can see it, grab it, and start creating

## The Image

The product image is what you're making content on. It's the first thing to look at — if it's something you can film well, that's half the decision.

## Category Tag

Each alert posts in its category channel and pings the matching role, so you know instantly which lane it's in. If it's in a category you didn't pick, you won't get pinged — that's the point of choosing your roles.

## Move Fast

An alert is a signal that a product is worth acting on now. The faster you go from alert to posted content, the more of the window you catch. Don't overthink it — check the product, and if it fits, move.
